## Week of Sunday, March 22nd 2026 To Saturday, March 28th 2026

The big thing in the tech world (maybe arguable the world) is  AI. I will spare you my opinion about the subject for now, but needless to sayeveryone is trying to adapt their process into AI. [DJ Sampath Run OpenClaw at Home, and that’s Exactly Why He Built DefenseClaw.](https://blogs.cisco.com/ai/cisco-announces-defenseclaw). Adding secure practice into AI is definitely a great approach, however  the approach feels more of  a patch than a well thought out solution. This is the same old adage, security is always a second thought when it come to technology.  In other words why OpenClaw didn't try to fix this, or have some sort of solution for this during its conception. DefenseClaw is basically adding a policy telling the AI in a sandbox environment what it can do. It seem like creating an AI framework with the help of AI, this would be at the top of the functional requirement. In any case, It wasn't. DefenseClaw approach sound good and without playing around with the tool I believe it can work.

While AI is continuously evolving, it's great to see how other field are improving their process. For example in [Data Centers Are Transitioning From AC to DC](https://spectrum.ieee.org/data-center-dc), we are seeing example of industry that are forced to improved to support the AI ecosystemy. This by itself would be a great take away, but I am still in surprised by the amount of energy AI consume. 

> Traditional data center computational racks draw on the order of 10 kW each. For AI, that is starting to approach 1 megawatt.

Maybe the solution lies in [AI doesn’t need more RAM but better math](https://adlrocha.substack.com/p/adlrocha-what-if-ai-doesnt-need-more). 

My parting thought is as follow, I am definitely on the fence about AI. It is not just a simple autocomplete tool on steroids. It has improved ange can get a lot of thing done. But on the other side the security concern, the energy cost and the unknown surrounding it just to name a few are reason to be a bit pessimist about the subject. We are definitely not a point of "just trust the ai", or even build large scale companies on the technologies. it's a bet companies are taking when trying to incorporate AI deeply into their process, a bet driven from a fear of missing out. It may end up being successful, but at this very moment it's a scary bet from everyone involved.
